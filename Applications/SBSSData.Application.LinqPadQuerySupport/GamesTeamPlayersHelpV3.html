In this page, we provide documentation for all the tables that appear in the actual Game Results, Team and Players page. Actual data is used, but just for one league to make navigation easier. Just click the "Show me" button to open text that describes the part of the table while viewing the table itself.

Game Results

The Game Results Data for the Monday Community Games table with all its nested tables encapsulates every bit of information that is in the data store. It also displays data calculated using the stored data. It is game-centric, meaning any row of the table has all the information for that played game. There's a row for each game. You can imagine that the table grows as more games are played. Click on the Scheduled Game "Show" button to expand that table in the first cell of the first row.

Scheduled Game

The first cell is the scheduled game information, which is determined before any games are played. If a game has not been played or has been canceled, it does not appear in the table — after all, there's no data!

Teams and Players

The second cell in the row, for example, Teams and Players for the Ann Fraley-Long Realty vs Ambient Air Heating & Cooling Game, actually encapsulates the results. Although these results are posted on the SSSA Web site, the values for team (and game) outcome are computed from the player "box line" values. This table obviously only has two rows, one for each team. For each team, the data for each player is the last cell of the row in this nested table. The rows are ordered as visitor's team, then home team, not by outcome.

Players for the Visitors

Now we're getting to heart of the data: It's all about player stats! You'll notice that the table, for example, Players for Ann Fraley-Long Realty, is much like what appears on the SSSA Web site when you use the "Schedules" menu item to view the results of a played game. There are some differences, however: All the usual calculated stats (average, slugging, etc.) are present. No question, that for a single game, this data are not very interesting or useful, but to keep the tables consistent with similar tables, these values are included. More interesting is that a new stat is added: Plate appearances (PA) which is just the number of official at bats plus bases on balls and sacrifice flies. The other difference is the summary line at the bottom that includes all player data and calculated stats.

Players for the Home Team

The second cell in the second row is just like the previous one, but it is for the home team rather than the visitors.

Team Summary Data & Standings

The second row in the main table, for example Team Summary Data and Standings for the 4 Monday Community Teams turns our perspective from games played to team performance. When the table is opened, a list of the teams in the league is presented in "standings" order (actually, by wins and then by run differential). You may notice, for example, that although "Andy's Irrigation-Andy McInnis" and "Barron Electric" have the same record, the former is in first place because the run differential (runs scored less runs against) is 25 versus -3 for "Barron Electric". (Full Disclosure: We do not know for certain that is how the SSSA handles tie won/loss records).

Team Players Summary

The last cell in each row is (yet) another table, but this time it's a cumulative summary of all players that played in any game for the team. The players that played 1,2 or 3 games are likely substitute players. But still they do contribute to the overall team stats, which are highlighted at the bottom of the table. Again, the line items for each player are the same when looking at a single game, but in this case the number games in which each player played is also presented. This data helps show the contribution players made to the success of their teams.

Player Summary Stats

In this last table, for example, Player Summary Stats for the 59 Players and Rankings for 47 Players With Enough Plate Appearances for All Teams, we really focus on the player. (N.B. The title may have different numbers because the Data Store may have been updated. You're looking at the latest data!) Here you see summary stats for every player in the league for all their activity on all teams. Because it's in a single league, it's reasonable to compare the stats. The player rows are sorted by the number of games played and then plate appearances. You open the last cell of a row to see a table of the ranking of the player for the four standard computed stats — Average (Avg), Slugging (Slug) On-Base percentage (OBP), and On-Base percentage plus slugging (OBS). Unlike the stats per game, these have real meaning.
Rather than opening the Ranking tables, just hover over the player name (second column) to see a pop-up of their photo and rankings. You can do that now!.
To calculate the rankings, a player must have had a minimal number of plate appearances. We've chosen 6 (likely having played in at least 2 games), but that's clearly subjective. The rankings are only based upon players who are qualified (that is, have had enough plate appearances). Finally the last row is the summary for all players. The league summary is not included in the calculation of the player ranks and is not ranked itself.
